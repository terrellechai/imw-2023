Process

Content Choice

Given the freedom to select content for the poster, I delved into a range of ideas inspired by both OCAD University and the Royal College of Art (RCA).

Typography Inspiration

The selection of Instrument Sans and Instrument Serif for the poster was a deliberate choice aimed at elevating the overall visual aesthetic. Instrument Sans, characterized by its modern and clean sans-serif design, imparts a sense of contemporary sophistication to the poster. Its streamlined and legible characteristics ensure the clear communication of essential information, contributing to a design that is both accessible and user-friendly. Conversely, Instrument Serif, distinguished by its traditional and elegant serif typeface, introduces a touch of refinement and timelessness to key elements of the poster. The combination of these two typefaces creates a harmonious contrast that facilitates the establishment of a visual hierarchy. Headlines and crucial details in Instrument Sans are presented boldly and straightforwardly, capturing attention, while Instrument Serif adds a layer of class and sophistication to important statements or secondary information. Together, Instrument Sans and Instrument Serif form a versatile typographic pairing, effectively complementing different aspects of the poster's content. This intentional selection enhances the overall cohesiveness and readability of the poster, ensuring that viewers can navigate and engage with the information seamlessly in a university setting.

Idea

In the creative process creating the poster, I embarked on an exploration of diverse ideas inspired by the distinctive approaches of renowned institutions such as OCAD University and the Royal College of Art (RCA), along with a playful touch drawn from the iconic Pac-Man. Navigating through OCADU's website and delving into the offerings of RCA, particularly their short course on "Human-Centred Design for Intelligent Mobility," became a crucial source of inspiration. This exploration aimed to infuse our event with the innovative perspectives fostered by OCADU's master's program, "Strategic Foresight and Innovation." The combination of these influential sources did not only help me with the idea of the landing page but also aimed to help me think how OCADs UI could seamlessly blend with RCAs

Code Application

After a thorough exploration involving six mockups, I have successfully identified the optimal design to proceed with for our project. The latest iteration boasts a refined and visually appealing layout, characterized by enhanced clarity and a strategic use of colors inspired by OCADU, coupled with the sophisticated black-and-white aesthetics drawn from RCA. In a conscious decision, I opted against utilizing the fonts associated with these institutions, as it manifested a deliberate departure from a conventional approach, contributing to a more distinctive design for the poster. While coding the HTML for the poster proved to be a relatively straightforward process, the challenge presented itself during the implementation of the CSS grid layout. In order to achieve a structured and versatile design, I crafted a 19-column grid accompanied by 41 rows. Leveraging the fraction method, such as (1 / 5), played a pivotal role in precisely placing the content within the grid, ensuring an organized and aesthetically pleasing composition.
